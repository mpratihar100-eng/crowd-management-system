# Privacy-First Person Detection - Privacy Policy

## Data Collection

Our system uses computer vision to count people in public spaces while maintaining strict privacy standards.

### What We Collect:
- ✅ **People count** (aggregate numbers only)
- ✅ **Anonymous position data** (centroid coordinates)
- ✅ **Crowd density metrics** (statistical aggregates)
- ✅ **Movement patterns** (anonymized flow data)

### What We DO NOT Collect:
- ❌ **No facial recognition**
- ❌ **No individual tracking across cameras**
- ❌ **No personal identification**
- ❌ **No video/image storage with faces**
- ❌ **No biometric data**
- ❌ **No personally identifiable information (PII)**

## Technical Privacy Measures

### 1. Background Subtraction
- Uses motion-based detection
- No facial feature analysis
- Anonymous blob counting

### 2. Immediate Anonymization
- Frames processed in real-time
- No raw video storage
- Only counts retained

### 3. Data Minimization
- Store only aggregate statistics
- No individual tracking IDs persist
- Automatic data expiration

### 4. GDPR Compliance
- No personal data processing
- No consent required (public space counting)
- Data subject rights not applicable (no PII)

### 5. Secure Processing
- All processing local/on-premise
- No cloud upload of video
- Encrypted data transmission

## Use Cases

✅ **Appropriate Uses:**
- Crowd density monitoring
- Queue management
- Safety capacity monitoring
- Resource allocation
- Traffic flow optimization

❌ **Prohibited Uses:**
- Individual identification
- Surveillance tracking
- Targeted advertising
- Law enforcement identification
- Discriminatory practices

## Data Retention

- **Real-time counts**: Immediate processing, no storage
- **Aggregate metrics**: 30 days (configurable)
- **Statistical reports**: 1 year (anonymized)
- **Video frames**: NEVER stored

## Compliance

- ✅ GDPR compliant (no personal data)
- ✅ CCPA compliant (no sale of data)
- ✅ HIPAA compliant (no health information)
- ✅ Ethical AI principles
- ✅ Privacy by design

## Transparency

This system is designed for counting people in public spaces without identifying individuals. It's equivalent to a human with a manual counter - no more privacy-invasive than that.
